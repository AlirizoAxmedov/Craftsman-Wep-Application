Qarshi Maktabi (Karshi School)

History

Karshi, an ancient city in southern Uzbekistan, developed its copper engraving tradition as part of the wider Silk Road economy. The city's position in the trade networks of the Kashka Darya region made it an important center for metalworking. The Karshi school developed distinctive techniques suited to the region's tastes and resources.

Distinctive Style

  • Emphasis on practical vessels and functional items
  • Clean, geometric patterns with clear lines and balanced compositions
  • Use of the "Rustami" technique creating three-dimensional shadow effects
  • Integration of regional motifs inspired by Kashka Darya's culture
  • Focus on durability and long-lasting artistic appeal

Characteristics

The Karshi school is known for its practical yet artistic approach to copper engraving. Master craftsmen of this region created pieces that were both functionally excellent and aesthetically pleasing. The designs tend toward clarity and geometric precision, avoiding excessive ornamentation in favor of elegant simplicity. Karshi pieces are celebrated for their durability and timeless appeal.

Notable Specialties

  • Large water vessels (Qazani and Aftobas) with decorative yet functional designs
  • Decorative plates combining artistic beauty with practical durability
  • Architectural fittings for traditional buildings
  • Small decorative items suitable for daily use

Legacy

The Karshi school's emphasis on combining beauty with functionality has made its pieces highly valued in traditional Uzbek households. The school's techniques continue to be practiced, with contemporary artisans preserving and adapting the tradition for modern purposes. Karshi's contribution to copper engraving lies in demonstrating that art and function need not be separate.
