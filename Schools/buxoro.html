Buxoro Maktabi (Bukhara School)

History

A center of Islamic culture and trade. In the mid-19th century, there were nearly 400 coppersmiths here, making Bukhara one of the most important centers of copper engraving in Central Asia.

Distinctive Style

  • Elegant and simple designs compared to other schools.
  • Famous for the "Chekma" technique (dotted background), which creates a distinctive textured appearance on the copper surface.
  • Patterns often mimic architectural motifs like mihrabs and tiles from Bukhara's famous monuments.
  • Introduced "Minoqoriy" (enamel painting on copper), adding vibrant colors to their designs.

Characteristics

The Bukhara school is known for its refined elegance and precise execution. Master artisans from Bukhara developed techniques that emphasized balance and symmetry, often incorporating floral patterns (Islimiy) with geometric precision. The use of the dotted background technique not only served an aesthetic purpose but also allowed craftsmen to create depth and dimension on flat copper surfaces.

Legacy

The Bukhara school's influence extended throughout Central Asia and beyond. Their techniques were studied and adapted by craftsmen in other regions, making Bukhara masters highly sought after for training apprentices.
